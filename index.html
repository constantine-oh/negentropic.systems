What do we owe each other? Not nothing.

On the Nature of the Work

Everything decays when no one is watching it. Systems drift, certificates lapse, the backups quietly stop running, and the universe, left to its own devices, settles toward a flat and final nothing. This is not a metaphor. It is the second law of thermodynamics, and it is undefeated.

Negentropic Systems exists to lose to it slowly, and on purpose, on behalf of other people.

Maintenance is a form of care. The universe tends toward nothing. We don't.
What That Means in Practice

We do the unglamorous work of keeping things from falling apart. Anyone who leans on a system — usually without ever knowing your name — deserves for it to hold. Holding it takes energy. Someone has to be willing to spend that energy, stay late, and push back against the dark.

Usually that someone has coffee. Here, that someone is us.

Who This Is For

This is support for the people doing strange, important work in the small hours. The ones rebuilding something, carrying something, watching something nobody else thought to watch. They get the same grave courtesy whether they run the place or just lost their password again.

What do we owe each other? After five-odd decades, I still can't hand you the whole answer. But I know it isn't nothing. So we start there, and we keep the lights on.
